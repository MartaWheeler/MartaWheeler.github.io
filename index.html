Background

Data Scientist with a mathematician's rigor

I'm a Lead Data Scientist specializing in capacity planning, demand forecasting, and operational optimization. My career spans banking (Citi), big tech (Meta), marketing analytics (Digitas), and healthcare — always at the intersection of advanced modeling and real business impact.

My foundation is in mathematics — M.Sc. from Wrocław University of Technology, where my thesis applied ARIMA and error-correction models to energy price prediction. That rigor shapes how I approach every problem: statistically sound, interpretable, and deployable.

I'm Polish-born, internationally educated (Poland, Germany), and US-based for over a decade. I bring a cross-cultural, cross-industry perspective to data science teams — and I genuinely love the craft of turning messy data into clear decisions.

What Drives Me

Research interests & passions

01
Energy Market Modeling
Rooted in my Master's thesis on electricity spot and futures price prediction using ARIMA, ARIMAX, and Error Correction Models. I'm fascinated by the interplay of seasonal patterns, geopolitical shocks, and long-run equilibrium in energy markets — and how classical statistical theory can capture all three.
02
Neural Networks & Deep Learning
From my Bachelor's ANN thesis on OCR to modern deep learning for forecasting — I'm excited by neural architectures that push beyond tabular ML. Particularly interested in temporal models like N-BEATS and Temporal Fusion Transformers for large-scale forecasting challenges.
03
Generative AI & LLM Applications
Actively building with LangChain agents, RAG pipelines, and fine-tuned LLMs. I'm interested in practical, grounded applications of Gen AI — turning natural language into code, automating analytics workflows, and building responsible AI systems.
04
Capacity Planning & Optimization
7 years at Citi gave me a deep appreciation for the stakes of infrastructure forecasting. I find the challenge of predicting demand under uncertainty — and converting model outputs into concrete resource allocation decisions — genuinely compelling.
05
Explainable AI & Model Interpretability
I believe models that can't be explained can't be trusted in production. My MycoNet project used Grad-CAM for CNN explainability; I advocate for SHAP and LIME in tabular ML. Interpretability isn't a constraint — it's a feature.
06
Computational Mathematics
My ECMI (European Consortium for Mathematics in Industry) education gave me a love for applied math — differential equations, numerical methods, optimization. I still find pure mathematical structure beautiful and return to it often when designing algorithms from scratch.
